Non-Corn-Pone Opinions

To understand what non-corn-pone opinions are, you must first understand what corn-pone opinions are.

Corn-Pone Opinions

Corn-Pone Opinions is the name of an essay Samuel Clemmens wrote under his pen name Mark Twain. First published in 1923, the short essay is easy to find online.

Corn-Pone Opinions describes an experience Twain had as a teenager living in a town on the banks of the Mississippi, years before the outbreak of the Civil War.

I had a friend whose society was very dear to me because I was forbidden by my mother to partake of it. He was a gay and impudent and satirical and delightful young black man — a slave — who daily preached sermons from the top of his master's woodpile, with me for sole audience.
 — From Corn-Pone Opinions by Mark Twin, 1923.

Early on in the essay, Twain quotes his friend, the black philosopher:

You tell me whar a man gits his corn pone, en I'll tell you what his 'pinions is.
 —  Corn-Pone Opinions.

This of course begs the question:

WTF Is Corn-Pone?

Corn-pone is used to make corn bread and it was an essential foodstuff in the 1850s.

In Twain's essay, though, corn-pone represents a specific type of self-approval.

Self-approval is acquired mainly from the approval of other people. The result is conformity. Sometimes conformity has a sordid business interest — the bread-and-butter interest — but not in most cases, I think. I think that in the majority of cases it is unconscious and not calculated.
 —  Corn-Pone Opinions. [Emphasis added.]

Corn-pone opinions are safe, conventional, and not worth sharing.

Non-Corn-Pone Opinions

The opposite of corn-pone opinions, non-corn-pone opinions are unconventional opinions.

The thing about it is, dear reader, writing safe, conventional, corn-pone opinions is a waste of time, because everyone already agrees with those.

Worse, in the majority of cases it is unconscious and not calculated.

Ground Rule

The rule here is, I can't care what you, or anyone else for that matter, thinks about my non-corn-pone opinions.

Looking for approval for my non-corn-pone opinions from anyone other than myself would make them corn-pone opinions!

Yikes, I wouldn't want to do that!!

Context

Non-corn-pone opinions, like their corn-pone counter parts, are of course contextual: they can vary over time and from place to place.

I live in Five Points, in Central Denver, Colorado, USA, and have been getting my corn-pone from the UnSafeway for nigh on 25 years now. Colorado is great: it's neither north nor south, nor east nor west. I grew up in Richmond Virginia, and spent about two years in LA.

Denver Colorado is not too big, and it's not too small: it's just exactly perfect!

Unfortunately, though, we're full, so don't move here. Thanks!

Now you know where I — and my corn-pone — are coming from. Just so you know!

Politics – A Perfect Example

As I write this in 2018, the two-party system in America has made politics farcical. Interestingly, Twain saw this sort of thing a hundred years ago:

[W]e know why Catholics are Catholics; why Presbyterians are Presbyterians; why Baptists are Baptists; why Mormons are Mormons; why thieves are thieves; why monarchists are monarchists; why Republicans are Republicans and Democrats, Democrats. We know it is a matter of association and sympathy, not reasoning and examination; that hardly a man in the world has an opinion upon morals, politics, or religion which he got otherwise than through his associations and sympathies.
 —  Corn-Pone Opinions.

Eating citified corn-pone makes people liberal, and liberals take views that are predictably liberal.

Eating countrified corn-pone makes people conservative, and conservatives take views that are predictably conservative.

Finding a Formula

When it comes to finding a formula for devising non-corn-pone opinions, the blending of the two, such as it is, in the suburbs and on the internet is not a concern. People are uniformly pissed off these days and more often than not willing to self-identify when pressed in any manner.

Men think they think upon great political questions, and they do; but they think with their party, not independently.... They swarm with their party, they feel with their party, they are happy in their party's approval; and where the party leads they will follow, whether for right and honor, or through blood and dirt and a mush of mutilated morals.
 —  Corn-Pone Opinions.

Despite the mixing of contexts that occurs online, which can be quite entertaining — in a tragic–comic sort of way — this divisiveness makes coming up with a non-corn-pone opinion a total no-brainer.

A political opinion can be non-corn-pone by simply being both liberal and conservative simultaneously — or by being neither liberal nor conservative.

Former FBI Director James Comey is on the right track in his article Why ‘Truth’ Is James Comey’s Word of the Year — but there are probably plenty who will claim he fell short of the mark because he didn't take a jab at one or both of the Clintons.

Well, pfft. Nobody's perfect.

Don't Assume!

This is not to say this site is full of non-partisan or bi-partisan opinions!

I am just saying:

  • Politics provides a huge stream of ready-made opinions, corn-pone-y and non-corn-pone-y alike
  • Today's divisive political climate provides us with a no-brainer formula for forming non-corn-pone opinions

Make no mistake, the focus of this site is not the content of this or that opinion, but rather the fact that — as Twain puts it in his essay — in the majority of cases it is unconscious and not calculated.

Eggs

First eggs were good, a staple of a modern breakfast. Then some researchers learned about cholesterol and suddenly eggs were bad.

Then some marketers wanted more money and decided they were good again.

If you're confused, now you can compromise and just eat the whites.

Rock 'n' Roll's Boring Parents

First there was jazz and R&B music for the black people, and popular and country music for white people. People played their music at home, and never the twain did meet.

Then the radio came along and the kids started listening to both types of music — and Rock 'n' Roll was born!

Dumping your assumptions can be quite liberating!

And sometimes a compromise — as in the case of the egg whites — can be easily found.
